AquaSentry

Autonomous Water Quality Monitoring & Community Empowerment

1. The Problem Statement

**Current State:** Many communities rely on local water sources with **no real-time safety check**.

Concrete Harms

  • Waterborne disease & long-term toxin exposure (arsenic, lead).
  • Lost work/school days & high medical spending.
  • Environmental and public health risks.

2. Root Causes

  • **Contamination:** Industrial/agricultural runoff into water bodies.
  • **Infrastructure Failure:** Aging/corroded distribution pipes.
  • **Testing:** Insufficient, costly, and distant local lab infrastructure.
  • **Operational:** Low user awareness and irregular maintenance.
  • **Logistics:** Power access issues at remote sites.

Overview: A solar-capable, battery-backed microcontroller node with pH/TDS/turbidity/temperature sensors, local OLED verdict display, and secure microSD logging, housed in a waterproof casing.

5. Detailed Hardware Component Roles

  • Sensors: pH, TDS, Turbidity, Temp (DS18B20). Placed in a PVC sleeve at the tap outlet.
  • Controller: Arduino Nano (or ESP32). The brain for reading, processing, and UI control.
  • Display & UI: 1.3â€³ OLED + 4 Tactile Buttons (Live, Prev/Next Log, Prev-Day).
  • Storage: MicroSD Module + 4-8 GB card for robust CSV logging.
  • Power System: 18650 Li-ion cell, TP4056 charger, MT3608 boost converter. Solar panel is optional for trickle charging.
  • Optional Enhancements: DS3231 RTC for accurate timestamping, buzzer/LED warning.

6. Installation

  • Mount Style: Attach to the pipe with a T-joint; probe sits in the flowing stream.
  • Box Location: Elevated beside tap, screen facing users, sealed with cable glands.
  • Solar: On nearby pole or tank roof for optimal sunlight exposure.

7. Software & Logic (Core Algorithms)

Sampling & Filtering: Hourly cadence (RTC), or on-demand 'Live'. Use median/trimmed mean of N quick samples to remove electrical/flow spikes.
Verdict Rules (Initial): Simple fixed thresholds: Safe (< 300 ppm TDS, pH 6.5â€“8.5, < 5 NTU Turbidity). Conservative rules for 'Washing Only' and 'Unsafe'.
Logging Format (CSV): YYYY-MM-DD,HH:MM,ph,tds,turbidity,temp,verdict,checksum

8. AI Options (Future)

  • On-Device Classifier: Small model (Decision Tree/Tiny NN) for more robust verdicts based on multi-sensor input vector.
  • Server/Cloud Model: Use larger models (if ESP32 connectivity added) to predict specific contamination types and analyze historical trends.
  • Explainability: Always display raw data; supplement AI verdict with the top contributing feature (e.g., "High TDS").

Validation, Operations, and Success Measurement

11. Testing & Validation

  • **Bench Tests:** Calibration using lab standard solutions (pH/TDS/turbidity standards).
  • **Field Validation:** Collect 50-200 parallel samplesâ€”device reading vs. certified lab tests.
  • **Durability:** Probe fouling acceleration, water splash, and dust ingress tests.
  • **User Testing:** Measure non-technical user comprehension time and error rate of the verdict display.

12. Maintenance & Ops

  • **Weekly:** Quick visual check and probe wipe (biofouling prevention).
  • **Monthly:** pH probe calibration with buffer solutions, SD file integrity check.
  • **Quarterly:** Deep cleaning, sacrificial probe tip replacement, and USB firmware update.
  • **Sustainability:** Train local community members for 20-minute field replacements.

16. Success Metrics

  • **Technical:** Verdict accuracy $\geq 90\%$; Uptime (MTBF); Battery autonomy (hours).
  • **Social:** Reduction in reported waterborne incidents in pilot area (%).
  • **Economic:** Cost per unit, total cost of ownership (TCO) over 3 years, community health cost savings.
